## Post 1: The narrative is shifting on AI

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_the-narrative-is-shifting-on-ai-have-you-activity-7464265192814706688-TOLZ  
**Reactions:** 246+ comments

The narrative is shifting on AI. Have you noticed?

We run a top community of marketing professionals and I've recently noticed a shift in the last 60 days. More voices getting louder and grumpier about AI stuff in May 2026 vs. March 2026.

They are tired of headlines like "you will be replaced by 2027" or "here's how I replaced our VP marketing with AI."

While AI provides incredible capabilities to marketers (ex: never login and deal with ads managers again), they don't want it at the cost of their jobs — or this idea that the role of marketing can be replaced by a computer.

Not giving a hot take or thought leadership — simply sharing an observation. More people in both work life and personal life beginning to push back on AI in the last 60 days.

## Post 2: AI is eating the B2B marketing funnel (Newsletter #249)

**Source:** Exit Five Newsletter, 21 May 2026  
**URL:** https://www.exitfive.com/newsletter/ai-is-eating-the-b2b-marketing-funnel

**Key themes:**

- Buyers now research via AI before ever filling out a lead form or contacting sales
- Marketers in 2026 may need to educate their company on how buying actually works now, then rebuild marketing around that motion
- Over-optimizing for only things you can tie directly to pipeline means missing the entire shift in buyer behavior
- Give up some short-term measurement to play for long-term gain — match marketing to buyer behavior, not what's easiest to put in a dashboard for the CFO

## Post 3: Four things working in B2B buying today (Newsletter #216)

**Source:** Exit Five Newsletter, 20 January 2026  
**URL:** https://exitfive.com/newsletter/ai-will-eat-b2b-buying-daves-newsletter

**What's working:**

1. **Hand-crafted events** — Exit Five hand-selected 100 people for an event and hand-wrote notes to all of them
2. **AI workflow automation** — Clay does research: LinkedIn summary, company summary, site activity
3. **Obsess over distribution** — Interview a customer, turn it into case study, social snippets, quotes for their LinkedIn; do the heavy lifting so it reaches their network too
4. **Start by talking to customers** — Real conversations beat assumptions

**What's not working:** Lead-gated content when buyers can get answers from ChatGPT without giving you their email.
