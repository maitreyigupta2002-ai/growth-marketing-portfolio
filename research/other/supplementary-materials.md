## 1. B2B YouTube Strategy: Turn a Small Audience Into Revenue

**Source:** The B2B Playbook (interview with Samu Kovacs)  
**URL:** https://theb2bplaybook.com/b2b-youtube-strategy  
**Type:** Long-form article / interview summary

**Key takeaways:**
- B2B YouTube is about getting in front of the right ICP at the moment they're searching — not chasing views
- Three content pillars: search-optimized videos, algorithm-based recommendations, bottom-of-funnel social proof
- YouTube + LinkedIn flywheel: take YouTube transcript and turn into full LinkedIn post (not summary or teaser)
- Send curated video playlists to prospects before sales calls to shorten cycles
- Timeline: 90 days for initial results; consistent lead gen compounds between 4–12 months
- Set up UTM links and self-reported attribution from day 1
- YouTube is more defensible against AI slop than text-based channels

## 6. Rob Walling — 5 Stages of Customer Awareness Framework

**Source:** MicroConf US 2024 talk + blog summaries  
**YouTube:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Sxn1ji7vYzU  
**Article:** https://relayto.com/blog/5-stages-of-awareness-b2b-content-strategy-guide-671f6d6ca21b3

**Framework (from Eugene Schwartz's Breakthrough Advertising):**
1. Unaware
2. Problem Aware
3. Solution Aware
4. Product Aware
5. Most Aware

**Application:** Early-stage B2B SaaS should prioritize solution-aware content (comparison pages, "best tool" lists) for higher conversion and faster time-to-revenue. Don't start intensive content marketing until first 10 customers.
